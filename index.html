About Me

I am Sayantan Dutta, a researcher with a multidisciplinary background in mathematics, physics, and computer science. Currently, I am a postdoctoral associate at Weill Cornell Medicine, Cornell University, New York, USA. My research interests span quantitative acoustic microscopy, quantum computing, quantum image processing, deep learning, and inverse problems.

Research Interests

  • Quantum Computing: Signal representation in a quantum computer, internal circuit noise removal, adaptive thresholding of a noisy quantum state.
  • Quantum Image Processing: Quantum mechanics-based adaptive transformation, image decomposition using quantum many-body interaction theory.
  • Deep Learning: Designing novel networks, deep unfolded networks, customized convolution kernels for inverse problems.
  • Quantitative Ultrasound: Study the acoustical and mechanical properties of soft tissues using quantitative acoustic microscopy.
  • Medical Image Processing: Analyze biomechanical properties of the sclera in myopic guinea pigs using ultrasound.
