I am Sayantan Dutta, a researcher with a multidisciplinary background in mathematics, physics, and computer science. Currently, I am a postdoctoral associate at Weill Cornell Medicine, Cornell University, New York, USA.

My research interests span quantitative acoustic microscopy, quantum computing, quantum image processing, deep learning, and inverse problems.

Research Interests:

Quantum Computing: Signal representation in a quantum computer, internal circuit noise removal in a quantum processor, and adaptive thresholding of a noisy quantum state.

Quantum Image Processing: Quantum mechanics-based adaptive transformation, image decomposition using quantum many-body interaction theory, image formation, and reconstruction.

Deep Learning: Designing novel networks, deep unfolded networks, and customized convolution kernels based on quantum mechanical laws for solving inverse problems.

Quantitative Ultrasound: Study the acoustical and mechanical properties of soft tissues at microscopic resolution for tissue characterization using quantitative acoustic microscopy.

Medical Image Processing: Analyze biomechanical properties of the sclera in myopic guinea pigs using very high-frequency ultrasounds and computed tomography image processing.
